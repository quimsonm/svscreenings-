**Subject:** "I feel fine" and "I am fine" aren't always the same sentence
**Preview:** A sonographer's honest take on getting older and why knowing matters.
**Send day:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 9-10am PT

Hi there,

This one's personal.

One of our sonographers sat down and wrote about what it's like to spend every week looking inside people's bodies — and how it changed the way they think about their own health.

It's not a sales pitch. It's not a scare piece. It's just honest.

---

**Nobody warns you about that part.** The slow stuff. The little moments where your body sends you a memo that says, "Hey. Just so you know. Things are different now."

Every week, we see people who feel fine. They look fine. They *are* fine, mostly. But every now and then, the screen shows us something they didn't know was there. A little narrowing. A little thickening. Something worth flagging.

And every time that happens, we think: *would I want to know?*

**Every single time, the answer is yes.**

Not because we're paranoid. But because **"I feel fine" and "I am fine" aren't always the same sentence.**
